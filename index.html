Boris Kopitović
🇲🇪 Montenegro Centre Forward 1.89m
"The most lethal striker in Singapore Premier League history wears red and black. The Jaguars just got their apex predator."

Tanjong Pagar United FC are proud to announce the arrival of Boris Kopitović — the man who redefined goalscoring in Singapore football. The Montenegrin forward is a name every SPL fan knows and fears. In 2022, he shattered records with an almost unbelievable 35 goals in 28 matches for Tampines Rovers, claiming the Golden Boot in the most dominant individual season the league has ever seen. He is Tampines' all-time leading scorer with 110 goals in 138 appearances — a club legend now writing his next chapter in Jurong East.

After a stint with Bali United in Indonesia, Boris returns to Singapore with unfinished business. His aerial power (1.89m), ruthless movement in the box, and ice-cold composure in front of goal make him the most complete striker available. The Jaguars now have their No.9.

Head Coach · Season 2026/27
Miquel "Miki" Lladó
🇪🇸 Spain · Sant Cugat del Vallès, Barcelona Head Coach
"I've coached across four continents — every experience brought me here. I'm ready to make Tanjong Pagar a name that resonates beyond Singapore."

Tanjong Pagar United FC are delighted to confirm the appointment of Miquel "Miki" Lladó Plana as Head Coach for the 2026/27 season. The 37-year-old from Barcelona brings a rare blend of Spanish tactical intelligence and global coaching experience, having shaped footballers and teams across Spain, India, the United States, and Norway.

Miki rose to prominence at CE Sabadell — one of Catalonia's most storied clubs — where he led the youth setup to the División de Honor Juvenil championship and guided the first team as Head Coach in LaLiga's lower professional pyramid. His methodology is rooted in positional play, high pressing, and physical preparation developed across every competitive level. Now, he brings that philosophy to Jurong East.

New Signing · Midfielder
Midfielder · 26 years old
Masahiro Sugita
🇯🇵 Japan · Aichi Prefecture Central Midfielder 1.64m
"A Singapore Premier League champion with razor-sharp technical ability — Sugita brings winning DNA to the Jaguars."

Tanjong Pagar United FC are thrilled to announce the signing of Japanese midfielder Masahiro Sugita ahead of the 2026/27 season. The 26-year-old from Nagoya brings something rare to Jurong East: he has already won the Singapore Premier League title. Sugita was part of the dominant Albirex Niigata (S) squad that clinched the 2022 SPL championship — he knows what it takes to win this league from the inside.

A graduate of Waseda University — one of Japan's most prestigious football academies — and a former Japan U18 international, Sugita combines intelligent movement, quick passing, and relentless energy in the engine room. Having sharpened his game further at Balestier Khalsa, the creative midfielder arrives at TPUFC with over 111 SPL appearances and the hunger to add more silverware to his collection. With Miki Lladó's positional system and Boris up front, Sugita is the link that makes it all work.

The Four Phases of Jaguar Football

01
⚔️

Organised Attack

Positional play with purpose. The Jaguars build patiently from the goalkeeper, occupying width and depth, seeking to disorganise the opposition before striking. Three attacking principles drive every move forward.

Offensive Transition

The moment the ball is won is the most dangerous in football. The Jaguars are trained to exploit disorganisation immediately — attacking the spaces behind the opposition press before they can recover.

Win → Attack Instantly Find Free Man Advance Same / Different Channel Be Vertical Attack Depth

"Steal — Depth. Win the ball and immediately go forward. No hesitation."

03

Organised Defense

Compact, aggressive, coordinated. Three defensive blocks — high, mid, low — each with specific roles, mechanisms and activation triggers. The entire team defends as a unit, with every player knowing exactly where to be.

04
🔄

Defensive Transition

The instant the ball is lost, every Jaguar reacts. The player who loses it is first to press. Others sprint back, close passing lanes and reform the defensive structure — zero time for the opponent to exploit the moment of loss.

Loser Presses First Counter-Press Sprint Recovery Runs Defensive Funnel Role Switch Immediately

"Lose the ball — protect yourself. 5 seconds to win it back or recover shape."

Pressing System

Three Pressing Blocks

🔴

High Press

Applied from the opposition's defensive third. The Jaguars press in numbers immediately after the opponent has the ball. Activation triggers are pre-set — every player knows when to go. The goal: win the ball in dangerous positions.

🟡

Mid Block

The default defensive shape. Compact between the lines, wide enough to defend channels. Constant block height, reactive pressure when the trigger appears. Forces the opponent wide and into low-percentage positions.

🔵

Low Block

When protecting a lead or facing elite opposition, the Jaguars drop into a tight defensive unit. Lines fuse, central lanes are locked, and every player is a defender. Controlled resistance — not passive defence.

Our Story

Five decades of Singapore football. The Jaguars have always fought.

1974 — Founded
Tanjong Pagar United FC is established. The Jaguars enter Singapore's football landscape, representing the Tanjong Pagar constituency in the heart of the city.
1982–1994 — Golden Era
Four President's Cup titles (1982, 1985, 1987, 1994) and two NFL Division One championships (1983, 1987). The Jaguars become one of Singapore football's most decorated clubs.
1998 — FA Cup Champions 🏆
Tanjong Pagar United lift the Singapore FA Cup. The club's greatest single-competition triumph and the pinnacle of their storied history.
2013–2025 — The Rebuild
The Jaguars re-enter the top flight of Singapore football after years of rebuilding. Competing in the Singapore Premier League, the club gradually restores its identity and community connection.
2026 — New Era 🐆
Boris Kopitović. Masahiro Sugita. Miki Lladó. The Jaguars have made the boldest moves in Singapore football. The 2026/27 season is not a rebuild — it's a statement.
